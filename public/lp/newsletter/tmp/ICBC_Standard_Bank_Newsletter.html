CHINA DAILY NEWS ROUNDUP

Wednesday, 04 November 2015

An Era of Low Rates

Beijing Review | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

Following a further slowdown in China's economic growth to 6.9 percent in the third quarter of 2015, the People's Bank of China (PBOC) launched the fifth reserve requirement ratio (RRR) reduction and the sixth round of interest rate cuts since last November. The PBC cut benchmark one-year lending and deposit rates by 25 basis points each to 4.35 percent and 1.5 percent respectively from October 24. Meanwhile, the RRR for all financial institutions was also cut by 50 basis points. The RRR now stands at 17 percent for large financial institutions and 13.5 percent for small and medium financial institutions.

PMI Figures Underscore Signs of Tentative Recovery

Caixin Online | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

The Caixin China Purchasing Managers' Index for the manufacturing and services sectors turned around to 48.3 and 52 respectively in October, from September's 47.2 and 50.5 levels. The services PMI continues to be above the neutral 50-point level for 120 months in a row. The Caixin Composite Output Index, which combines the two, was 49.9 in October, only marginally below the neutral level and up 1.9 points from September.

China Lowers Expectations for Growth

Wall Street Journal Asia | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

China's President sent the strongest signal yet that the government expects the world's second-largest economy to shift to a slower pace, suggesting the economy could tolerate growth as low as 6.5%

Will 13th five-year plan mark a new start for China's development?

South China Morning Post | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

The guidelines for the draft of the 13th five-year plan, which begins in 2016 were approved. President Xi Jinping has repeatedly called for adaptation to a "new normal" slower growth to make room for structural economic reforms. As part of his main political agenda for his 10-year tenure, Xi wants to see a doubling of China's GDP per capita by 2020 from the 2010 level. To realise that goal, the government should at least strive to achieve an annualised growth rate of 6.5 per cent in the coming five years.

Chinese commercial lenders' bad loan ratio rises

Marketwatch | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

China's banking regulator said that the bad loan ratio of the nation's commercial lenders rose to 1.59 percent at the end of September from 1.5 percent at the end of June, as the slowing economy continues to take a toll on the financial system. The loan loss provision ratio declined to 167.7 percent at the end of September, from 198.4 percent three month earlier, according to the regulator.

IMF postpones SDR Evaluation

China Finance | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

The International Monetary Fund has postponed its decision on whether to include the Yuan in the Special Drawing Rights (SDR) basket until Nov. 30. The IMF was originally planning to make a decision on Nov. 4. The market expects IMF to include Yuan in the SDR currency basket.

Chinese Yuan weakens to 6.3343 against USD Wednesday

Xinhua | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

The central parity rate of the Chinese currency Renminbi, or the Yuan, weakened by 33 basis points to 6.3343 against the U.S. dollar on Wednesday, according to the China Foreign Exchange Trading System.

HK could be the best test ground for RMB globalization

Xinhua | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

Hong Kong Financial Secretary said Hong Kong could play a crucial role in RMB globalization as the most important RMB offshore center.

Asia Pacific Market: Stocks rejoice on strong offshore lead

Business Standard | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

Asia Pacific share market soared up on Wednesday, 04 November 2015, as risk sentiments boosted up on positive lead from offshore markets overnight, surge in commodity prices, and on speculation the region's policymakers would keep stimulus taps open, with Mainland China market leading regional rally after the government unveiled its five-year plan to bolster the economy. Hong Kong stock market surged, as risk sentiments buoyed by speculation that Chinese authorities will open a trading link between the two cities by year-end.

Shenzhen-Hong Kong connect false start sparks stock rally

FT | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing briefly became the world’s most valuable bourse by market capitalisation after an article posted online by Zhou Xiaochuan, the head of the People’s Bank of China, appeared to confirm that a stock link between Hong Kong and Shenzhen would begin by the end of this year. But HKEx shares gave back more than half their 9 percent rally after it was clarified that the newly published piece was based on a speech made by Mr Zhou in May, when China’s markets were still shy of their June 12 peak.

China's big four insurers see net profit surge for Jan-Sep

China Knowledge Press | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

China's big four insurers realized a total net profit of RMB 105.8 billion in the first nine months of 2015, accounting for 43.4% of the industrial total, according to the financial reports released by the insurers.

China Vanke 's property sales up 7.1% YoY in Oct

China Knowledge Press | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

China Vanke Co. Ltd, the country's largest listed residential property developer by market value, has announced that its property sale hit RMB 23.65 billion in October, posting a growth of 7.06% year on year and declining 3.15% month on month.

China Life Insurance Invests More Than $1 Billion in U.S. Logistics Property

Dow Jones Newswires Chinese (English) | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

State-owned China Life Insurance Co. is investing more than $1 billion in U.S. warehouses, according to a person with knowledge of the deal, marking its largest cross-border real-estate purchase to date. China Life will take about a 30% stake in warehouse operations that have recently been acquired by Singapore-listed Global Logistic Properties Ltd.

U.S. Sets New Duties on Chinese Steel

Dow Jones Newswires Chinese (English) | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

The U.S. Department of Commerce slapped preliminary duties of up to 236% on imports of corrosion-resistant steel from China. The tariff goes into effect immediately and will be set for five years if a final ruling in favor of the duty is made in January.

China underreporting coal consumption by up to 17%

The Guardian | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

Official Chinese data, reported by the New York Times on Wednesday after being quietly released earlier this year, suggests China has been burning up to 17% more coal each year than previously disclosed by the government. In 2012 China consumed 600m more tonnes of coal – or more than 70% of the United States’ annual total – than previously disclosed, according to the revised data. The revelation could complicate the fight against global warming ahead of the United Nations climate change conference in Paris, which begins on 30 November.

China gold consumption rises 7.8 pct

Xinhua Finance | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

China's gold consumption rose 7.83 percent year on year to 814 tonnes in the first three quarters. The rebound followed the 24.7 percent plunge last year, according to data released by China Gold Association (CGA).

China lowers gasoline, diesel prices

China Knowledge Press | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

China's National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) cut the retail price of gasoline and diesel each by RMB 125 per ton, effective from Nov 4. It has been the tenth cut within this year. The retail price of #90 gasoline and that of diesel was trimmed by RMB 0.09 and RMB 0.11 per liter, respectively.

Agricultural imports from Vietnam see rapid growth

China Daily | Wednesday, 04 November 2015

China imported $2.11 billion worth of agricultural products from Vietnam between January and September, up 23.1 percent year-on-year, according to the country's General Administration of Customs. Meanwhile, China shipped $2.39 billion worth of agricultural products to Vietnam, an increase of 15.3 percent from the same period last year. Vietnam is China's sixth largest market for agricultural product export and the 10th biggest import source.
